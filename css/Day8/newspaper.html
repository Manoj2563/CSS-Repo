India's Chandrayaan-3 moon lander
successfully separates

The Indian Space Research Organisation (Isro) has announced that the Lander Module of Chandrayaan-3 is set to separate from the Propulsion Module today. The separation will be conducted between 1:00 and 1:30 PM.Chandrayaan-3, launched on July 14, consists of a lander module (LM), a propulsion module (PM), and a rover. The spacecraft successfully entered lunar orbit on August 5, following which a series of orbit reduction maneuvers were carried out to position it over the lunar poles.Today's separation of the Lander Module from the Propulsion Module will mark the completion of the lunar-bound maneuvers ahead of the soft landing. The successful firing, needed for a short duration, on August 16 had put Chandrayaan-3 into an orbit of 153 km x 163 km, as intended.Following the separation, the Lander Module will undertake the rest of the journey to the Moon's surface independently. It is expected to undergo a "deboost" process, slowing down to place it in an orbit where the Perilune (closest point to the Moon) is 30 kilometers and Apolune (farthest point from the Moon) is 100 km.
